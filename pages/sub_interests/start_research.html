START Services

Using data to promote better healthcare

Research

Hover over the following sections to understand the goals, findings, and source code.

  • Introduction

    The National Center for Start Services Institute on Disability at the University of New Hampshire addresses the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on mental health patients. Research examines the secondary effects of COVID-19, such as isolation and boredom, on START participants' mental health. Evaluating the START initiative's effectiveness during the pandemic, the study analyzes patient behavior assessment and involvement time. Data from January 2019 to December 2020 reveals a 10% decrease in patient scores and a 38.46% reduction in average START staff time with patients. The research also aims to visualize COVID-19's effect on START participants' caregivers.
