A Creative Outlet

Background

I walked into the room towards the back, where our finished projects would be found. I see my picture frame, dried from the stain I brushed onto it the day prior. I took a second to congratulate myself for the detailed work in creating a perfect yet simple wooden picture frame. Comprised only from the most basic resources, I made something, and it felt inspiring. That was the day a new passion was born in me, and all it took was a rewarding 7th-grade woodworking workshop. Ever since I couldn't stop myself from wanting to understand the world around me, it didn't matter if I was walking down the street or in the backseat of my mother's car; I always thought about machines and their parts. I took engineering classes during summers to continue my interest in the field. I used the money I saved up to buy subscriptions to car magazines. I started a woodworking shop in my garage with a friend. Little did I know, it became more than a superficial interest.

Over the years, woodworking has become a creative way to express my interests and build beautiful yet functional pieces. Whenever I find myself with spare time, I like to spend it in the garage working on my next project. Just recently, I began a tableside book collection and needed some bookends. Instead of purchasing them, I thought it would be fun to design and construct custom pieces that reflect my other interests. A preview can be found below.

Windsor Custom Creations

When schools shut down due to COVID-19, I utilized the extended summer break to launch a woodworking business. Initially, recognition was low, and customer numbers were limited. Over time, as we showcased various projects, public curiosity grew. Through word of mouth, we accumulated customers for custom projects, working within tight budgets and deadlines. We designed and built custom picture frames, tables, storage containers, and other wood projects. Some are showcased in the gallery below, with the largest projects being a coffee table and a four Mac computer cooling station. Most profits were reinvested in the business for new equipment and tools.
