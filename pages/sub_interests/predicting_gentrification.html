Predicting Gentrification

Undergraduate Thesis

Introduction

Gentrification is a complex urban phenomenon that has long been studied due to its extensive social, economic, and cultural implications. It can be summarized as the transformation of neighborhoods from low-income to high-income areas, which often results in the relocation of long-term residents. This senior thesis will initially explore previous methods in predicting gentrification growth, which would use historical census data, housing market trends, infrastructure investments, and demographic changes. As this topic has been heavily researched, there are a plethora of research papers online. Then, the thesis will expand upon the previous standard and use computer vision algorithms to detect visual changes in different areas. This will be done using Google Street View, which provides the ability to view high-quality street-level photos over the past 15 years. This spatial analysis can reveal changes and trends that might not be evident from field-sourced data alone. By integrating street view data with traditional sources, more comprehensive predictive models can be built that consider both the physical and socioeconomic factors, potentially leading to more accurate predictions.
